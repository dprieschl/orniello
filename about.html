The Maker

Lydia Valant

Archaeologist · Jewelry Artist · Reenactor

The beginning

Jewelry and history have been my passion since childhood. At the age of 13 I made my first metal jewelry piece; in my early twenties I earned my first money selling them at medieval fairs. Though I started with LARP and fantasy jewelry, as the years went on I became more interested in historical styles.

Now, my focus is on reenactment and the recreation of historical jewelry and accessories. I'm still working at medieval fairs and museum events - you can see the dates here. For booking me and my booth, please note that due to bureaucratic reasons I'm staying in Austria for now.

Inspiration & craft

I get inspired by archaeological finds or historical paintings from all over Europe and beyond, and think that a lot of jewelry from former times could be incorporated into modern fashion. Some styles were even en vogue for centuries - you wouldn't recognize the design as "old". My creations are direct copies of or highly inspired by historical examples but also modern interpretations of historical pieces.

Materials

The materials I use are silver, (real) bronze, and 14K-24K gilded brass, as well as standard findings, freshwater pearls, gemstones, and glass beads. Some pieces and materials are vintage and re-used in my designs, so a lot of my jewelry is unique and cannot be reproduced.
